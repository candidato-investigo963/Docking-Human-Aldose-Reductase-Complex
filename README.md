# Docking-Human-Aldose-Reductase-Complex

This repository presents a specialized structural bioinformatics project focused on the molecular docking of Human Aldose Reductase (ALR2), a 36 kDa enzyme and a key pharmacological target for diabetic complications. The study utilizes the high-resolution crystal structure from PDB: 2FZD (1.08 Å) to analyze the binding efficiency of the inhibitor Tolrestat. 
By employing a flexible-residue docking approach (focusing on Ser302), the research explores the stabilization of the protein-ligand complex through hydrogen bonds, salt bridges with Lys77 and His110, and Van der Waals interactions. The analysis successfully predicts the binding orientation with a binding energy of -8.85 kcal/mol, showing high conformational stability across 100 docking runs.

The repository includes the full technical report (PDF), along with high-quality visualization assets. You will find PNG images detailing the clustering analysis and molecular interaction maps, as well as the CIF files for the protein and ligand structures used in the simulations.
